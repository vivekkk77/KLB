We Understand What You’re Worried About.

Relocating a pet is not just a logistical challenge; it is a significant responsibility. We recognize that ensuring your pet’s safety requires immense trust, which is why we address every concern with transparency.

We exclusively partner with airlines that strictly adhere to IATA Live Animal Regulations (LAR). Your pet travels in a temperature-controlled, pressurized cargo hold designed specifically for the transport of live animals. We ensure that the travel crate meets all international size, ventilation, and safety standards.

Delays usually stem from incomplete filings. As a licensed Customs House Agent, we conduct rigorous pre-clearance checks of all required health certificates, import permits, and vaccination records before the flight departs. We anticipate regulatory requirements to ensure a seamless process upon arrival.

International relocation requires precise adherence to the destination country's protocols. Our team conducts a thorough review of every document against current government regulations to ensure full compliance. By verifying all paperwork prior to travel, we significantly mitigate the risk of administrative rejection.

While transit times vary, minimizing duration is our priority. We coordinate closely with cargo terminals to ensure prompt check-in and expedited retrieval. Our team monitors the shipment status continuously to initiate clearance procedures immediately upon the flight's arrival.

According to IATA regulations, sedation is generally not recommended as it may inhibit a pet’s ability to regulate body temperature and balance during turbulence. We advise against the use of sedatives unless explicitly prescribed by your veterinarian for specific medical reasons.

Our role is to eliminate uncertainty at every step.

You handle the cuddles. We handle the regulations.

IATA Compliance

How To Measure Your Pet

An accurate measurement is essential to ensure that your dog travels in the correct size carrier. This is a mandatory requirement for dogs travelling as cargo or excess baggage with their owner.

The wrong size may not be accepted by the airline. Kennels that do not meet these dimension criteria or the IATA Live Animal Regulations (LAR) requirements will be refused uplift.

Note: Please DO NOT include the length of the tail while measuring.

  • Start making your pet used to the crate / crate training several weeks before travel.
  • The pet must be able to do a complete turn inside the box and when seated must have a minimum of 2 inches of space above the head.
  • The crate must have a drinker (water bottle) & feeder bowl attached to the door.
  • The crate must have absorbent material on the floor.
  • The box must have screws or nut bolts on all sides of the crates. Twist locks are not acceptable.
  • The pet’s nose or paws should not be able to fit through any ventilation opening or door mesh.

The size of the carrier must be such that it will allow your pet to stand in a natural position, turn around and sit down comfortably.
